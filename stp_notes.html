1. Introduction

STP is a fundamental marketing process:

  • Segmentation: Divide market into groups
  • Targeting: Select which groups to serve
  • Positioning: Build product image in customer mind

2. Market Segmentation

Definition: Dividing market into smaller, similar consumer groups.

Bases of Segmentation:

  • Geographic (region, city, rural/urban)
  • Demographic (age, gender, income, occupation)
  • Psychographic (lifestyle, values, personality)
  • Behavioural (benefits sought, loyalty, usage rate)

Example (India): Coca Cola – different SKUs (200 ml glass bottle for rural market, large PET bottles for urban families)

3. Targeting

Definition: Choosing which segments to serve.

Targeting Strategies:

  • Undifferentiated (mass marketing)
  • Differentiated (different products for different segments)
  • Concentrated (focus on one segment, niche)
  • Micromarketing (individual or local marketing)

Example (India): Parle-G uses undifferentiated targeting (same product for masses). Himalaya Baby Care uses concentrated targeting (new mothers).

4. Positioning

Definition: Creating a clear, distinctive, desirable place in the minds of target consumers compared to competitors.

Steps in Positioning:

  • Identify competitive advantages
  • Select the right advantage
  • Communicate it effectively

Example (India): Volvo positions on safety and reliability. Maggi positions as quick, tasty, and emotional connect with '2-minute noodles'.

5. Importance of STP

  • Helps focus marketing resources
  • Matches products to customer needs
  • Creates strong brand image
  • Drives better sales and profitability
